01 / 05 HELIOS

The H inscribed within a circular frame — a direct reference to the Helios moon base ring. The circle reads as an orbital structure; the H is its core. One colour, infinite scale. Think NASA mission patch meets Hyperion corporation.

02 / 05 MARK

The Hyperion H — redrawn properly. Thick pillars (16px each), tight gap (8px), crossbar at the optical centre. Nothing added, nothing removed. The kind of mark that looks like it was always there. Flat yellow, zero decoration. This is how IBM, FedEx, and Nike do it.

03 / 05 ARC

An orbital arc sweeps from the top of the left pillar to the top of the right, like a satellite path above the Helios station. The arc is yellow, the H below it is white. Two elements, two weights, one mark. Feels like aerospace, communications, reach.

04 / 05 AXIS

The H crossbar runs at a 23° diagonal instead of horizontal. The two pillars stay perfectly vertical — the tension between them creates energy. Reads immediately as H, but the slash crossbar makes it feel aggressive and forward-moving. Borrowed from Hyperion's weapon and UI branding language.

05 / 05 MONO w/ cyan accent

Two heavy white pillars. One thin cyan bar. That's the entire mark. The tension between the massive pillars and the surgical crossbar creates visual power. The cyan crossbar is the only colour — it's the signal, the AI, the intelligence connecting the two sides. Used by companies that don't need to explain themselves.
